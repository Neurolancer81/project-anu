# Campaign Structure

The 13-encounter campaign across the four Yugas.

---

## Campaign Overview

### Total Encounters: 13
- **Per Yuga:** 3 encounters (intro, development, Yugantara)
- **Final Battle:** 1 encounter (vs alternate selves)
- **Minimum playthrough:** All 13 encounters
- **Optional side content:** Investigation scenes with skill checks

### Playtime Estimate
- **First playthrough:** 10-15 hours
- **Per encounter:** ~45-60 minutes average
- **Investigation between missions:** 5-10 minutes each

---

## Yuga Structure

### Four Yugas (Hindu Cosmic Cycles)

**1. Satya Yuga (Golden Age)**
- Era of truth, righteousness, dharma
- Visual: Bright, pristine temples, lush nature
- Threat level: Early/tutorial difficulty
- 3 encounters

**2. Treta Yuga (Silver Age)**
- Decline begins, conflict emerges
- Visual: Grand kingdoms, fortifications, organized armies
- Threat level: Moderate difficulty
- 3 encounters

**3. Dvapara Yuga (Bronze Age)**
- Moral degradation, widespread war
- Visual: Darker tones, war-torn landscapes, corruption
- Threat level: High difficulty
- 3 encounters

**4. Kali Yuga (Iron Age)**
- Age of strife, deception, apocalypse
- Visual: Ruined civilization, apocalyptic wasteland
- Threat level: Very high difficulty
- 3 encounters

**Final Battle**
- Location: Outside time/space
- Enemy: Alternate version of player's squad
- Difficulty: Maximum

---

## Per-Yuga Encounter Flow

### Encounter 1: Introduction
- **Purpose:** Introduce yuga setting and era-specific threats
- **Length:** Standard combat encounter
- **Narrative:** Initial deployment, mysterious events

### Encounter 2: Development
- **Purpose:** Escalate threat, reveal more of the mystery
- **Length:** Standard combat encounter
- **Narrative:** Uncover clues about the "evil force"

### Encounter 3: Yugantara Battle
- **Purpose:** Climactic end-of-yuga battle
- **Length:** Longer, more complex encounter
- **Narrative:** Reveal Asuras appear at yuga transition
- **Special:** Higher difficulty spike, dramatic encounter design

---

## Narrative Progression

### Act 1: The Mission (Satya Yuga)
**Setup:**
- Player recruited as commander of secret society
- Mission: Discover when/how the world ends
- Deploy to pivotal moments in Satya Yuga

**Encounters:**
1. Initial deployment, encounter corrupted beings
2. Investigate source of corruption
3. Yugantara: Witness first yuga transition event

**Revelation Tier 1:** There's a force manipulating events across time

---

### Act 2: The Pattern (Treta Yuga)
**Setup:**
- Pattern emerges: Evil force active in each yuga
- Deploy to critical moments in Treta Yuga
- Investigate historical "Asura" legends

**Encounters:**
4. Deploy to conflict zone, mysterious enemies
5. Encounter evidence of recurring threat
6. Yugantara: Second yuga transition, Asuras referenced

**Revelation Tier 2:** Asuras appear at each yuga-end, causing transitions

---

### Act 3: The Truth (Dvapara Yuga)
**Setup:**
- Evidence mounts: Asuras are connected to player's squad
- Disturbing similarities in descriptions
- Deploy to Dvapara's pivotal moments

**Encounters:**
7. Encounter survivors who describe "saviors turned destroyers"
8. Face enemies who recognize the squad (impossible?)
9. Yugantara: Heavy foreshadowing, squad questions reality

**Revelation Tier 3:** Mounting evidence player squad ARE the Asuras

---

### Act 4: The Cycle (Kali Yuga)
**Setup:**
- Truth revealed or heavily implied
- Player squad realizes they're causing the yuga transitions
- Deploy to final yuga, knowing the paradox

**Encounters:**
10. Kali Yuga deployment, apocalyptic setting
11. Confront the truth, try to break the cycle
12. Yugantara: Final yuga transition, squad embraces role

**Revelation Tier 4:** Full reveal - player IS the Asura

---

### Act 5: The Mirror (Final Battle)
**Setup:**
- Outside normal time/space
- Face alternate version of your own squad
- Are you fighting past/future selves? A reflection? The cycle itself?

**Final Encounter (13):**
- Mirror match: Your squad vs identical alternate squad
- Same classes, same abilities, same tactics
- Narrative conclusion: Break the cycle or perpetuate it?

**Endings:**
- **Victory:** Break the cycle (or complete it?)
- **Defeat:** Game over, cycle continues
- Potential for multiple interpretation endings

## Investigation System (Between Encounters)

### Menu-Based Investigation
After each combat encounter, return to **Investigation Menu**.

### Investigation Scenes
- **Static backgrounds:** 2D art of location
- **Skill check prompts:** Use squad member skills to investigate
- **Clue discovery:** Success reveals narrative clues or tactical advantages

### Skill Checks
- Select squad member with highest relevant skill
- Roll d20 + skill modifier
- **Success:** Discover clue, lore, or unlock advantage
- **Failure:** Miss optional information (not blocking)

**Common skills:**
- **Perception:** Notice details, hidden evidence
- **Arcana:** Understand magical phenomena
- **Religion:** Interpret rituals, divine signs
- **Society:** Historical context, cultural knowledge
- **Diplomacy/Intimidation:** Extract information from NPCs

### Investigation Rewards
- **Narrative clues:** Advance understanding of Asura mystery
- **Tactical intel:** Enemy composition for next encounter
- **Resources:** Equipment, items for squad (TBD based on meta-layer scope)

### Required vs Optional
- **At least 1 investigation per Yuga** required to unlock Yugantara
- **Additional investigations** provide advantages but are optional

## Permadeath & Failure

### Permadeath
- Unit death is permanent
- Recruit replacements at HQ (between missions)
- New recruits level scales (TBD: Start at level 1 or campaign level?)

### Mission Failure
- **Failure condition:** All squad members defeated
- **Consequence:** Mission failed, game over
- **Reload:** Restart encounter

**Alternative (TBD):**
- Mission retreat option? (XCOM-style)
- Partial failure with consequences?

## HQ & Between-Mission Flow

### Mission Flow
1. **Return to HQ** after encounter
2. **Manage wounded** (if any survived with injuries)
3. **Recruit replacements** for dead units
4. **Investigation phase** (skill checks, clues)
5. **Meta-layer activities** (research/engineering, TBD)
6. **Select squad** for next mission (4-6 units)
7. **Deploy** to next encounter

### HQ Features (Confirmed)
- **Barracks:** View roster, recruit new soldiers
- **Mission select:** Choose next encounter
- **Squad loadout:** Equip units before mission

### HQ Features (TBD - Awaiting Meta-Layer Scope Decision)
- **Research tree?**
- **Engineering/weapon upgrades?**
- **Resource economy?**
- **Wounded recovery time?**

## Side Content

### Optional Investigations
- Extra investigation scenes per Yuga
- Provide lore, resources, tactical advantages
- Not required for campaign progression

### Replayability
- Different class compositions
- Different investigation choices
- Speedrun potential (minimum 13 encounters)
- Ironman mode (single save, permadeath stakes higher)

## Asset Requirements Per Yuga

### Satya Yuga
- Bright, golden aesthetic
- Temple/palace architecture
- Lush natural environments
- **Generic asset pack:** Fantasy golden age/heavenly realm

### Treta Yuga
- Grand medieval kingdoms
- Fortifications, castles
- Organized armies aesthetic
- **Generic asset pack:** Medieval/high fantasy

### Dvapara Yuga
- War-torn, darker aesthetic
- Corrupted/damaged environments
- Conflict aftermath
- **Generic asset pack:** Dark fantasy/ruins

### Kali Yuga
- Apocalyptic wasteland
- Ruined civilization
- Desolate, hopeless aesthetic
- **Generic asset pack:** Post-apocalyptic/wasteland

### Final Battle
- Abstract/otherworldly space
- Mirror/reflection theme
- **Custom or minimal:** Could be arena-style
